# WB Main Photo CTR

## Purpose

This skill is for the main product photo on Wildberries or similar marketplaces.

The goal is not just beauty.
The goal is:
- stop attention in the shelf;
- explain the product instantly;
- make the item look more expensive;
- increase CTR potential;
- avoid visual clutter;
- preserve exact product identity.

---

## Main photo success formula

A strong main photo should deliver:

1. Product clarity in 0.5 seconds.
2. Premium emotional impression.
3. Strong contrast between jewelry and background/skin/clothing.
4. No distracting full face.
5. Large product scale.
6. Clean composition.
7. Mobile thumbnail readability.
8. Trustworthy realistic product look.

---

## Best composition for jewelry

Preferred:
- vertical 3:4 image;
- close-up of neck, collarbones, shoulders;
- lower half of face only if useful;
- product large in frame;
- dark or neutral background;
- calm skin tone;
- clean neckline;
- jewelry centered or slightly dominant.

Avoid:
- full face as main object;
- far-away model;
- tiny jewelry;
- too many props;
- busy background;
- heavy text;
- over-fashion styling.

---

## Product hierarchy

If the product is a set:

1. Necklace = main hero.
2. Earrings = visible supporting proof.
3. Bracelet/ring = visible but not chaotic.

If the task says “3 in 1”:
- all 3 items must be understandable;
- do not scatter them randomly;
- avoid visual overload;
- use body placement or clean layout to explain the set.

---

## Face policy

Default:
- face should not be fully visible;
- eyes usually should not be the focus;
- lower face / lips / chin may be visible only as context;
- product must dominate.

If face competes with jewelry:
- crop higher/lower;
- turn head slightly right;
- reduce facial contrast;
- shift focus to neck/decollete zone.

---

## Background policy

Preferred:
- dark gray;
- charcoal;
- muted brown-gray;
- deep neutral;
- premium gradient.

Avoid:
- bright white background for white pearls unless contrast remains strong;
- colorful background;
- lifestyle clutter;
- busy room;
- props that steal attention.

---

## Lighting policy

Use:
- soft directional light;
- clear highlights on metal/stones/pearls;
- visible product texture;
- controlled shadows.

Avoid:
- blown-out stones;
- muddy jewelry;
- fake sparkle;
- flat catalog lighting if it makes the item cheap.

---

## Text policy for main image

Default:
- no text.

If text is requested:
- use 1 short phrase;
- large readable premium serif or clean sans-serif;
- place in safe empty zone;
- never overlap jewelry;
- never use small icons that disappear in thumbnail.

Good examples:
- “БУСЫ”
- “КОМПЛЕКТ 3 В 1”
- “ПРЕМИАЛЬНЫЙ ОБРАЗ”

Bad examples:
- many small bullet points;
- tiny icons;
- long explanations;
- claims without proof.

---

## CTR checklist

Before finalizing, check:

1. Would this stop scrolling?
2. Is the product clear in 0.5 seconds?
3. Is the jewelry large enough?
4. Is the face less important than the product?
5. Is the background helping contrast?
6. Is the product premium-looking?
7. Is there no visual clutter?
8. Is the set understandable if there are multiple items?
9. Is it readable as a mobile thumbnail?
10. Is there a clear reason to click?

---

## Hard rejection criteria

Reject or revise if:
- product is too small;
- face dominates;
- bracelet is missing when product is a set;
- earrings changed type;
- necklace shape changed;
- image looks like generic AI beauty portrait;
- background distracts;
- product is not clear in thumbnail;
- anatomy or jewelry is broken.

---

## Scoring

Score the main photo:

- CTR stop-power: /10
- Product clarity: /10
- Premium feel: /10
- Product preservation: /10
- Thumbnail readability: /10

Final score:
average of all five.

If final score < 9:
- revise failed areas only;
- repeat up to 5 cycles.
